01 Why now

Why now, and why this fits

PortSwigger is early in a serious internal AI transformation, and hiring a new kind of team to drive it. The AI Pioneer brief describes the job precisely: embed with a team, find where the friction really is, build something that works, evaluate it honestly, hand it over, move on.

Separately, the UK government designed an apprenticeship standard for exactly this moment. The Level 4 AI and Automation Practitioner programme exists so employers can build internal AI and automation capability by training someone on their real work, inside the business, with the training bill covered.

Put the two side by side and the overlap is hard to ignore.

02 The win-win

One arrangement, two clear wins

An apprenticeship is not a favour to the apprentice. Structured properly, it is a low-cost, low-risk way for PortSwigger to add building capacity while the government pays for the training.

The PortSwigger side

What PortSwigger gets

  • A builder embedded on real internal automation problems from month one, not after graduation.
  • Each month's learning applied to your work the same month it is taught.
  • Apprentice terms: genuine capability at a fraction of the senior band you advertised.
  • Training funded by government, worth up to £18,000, at little to no new cost.
  • A structured 15-month engagement with monthly reviews and a named coach. Low risk by design.
  • A byproduct: structured AI upskilling for the wider team through short share-back sessions.

My side

What I bring, and what I gain

  • Nine months of real, live AI builds pointed at your problems from day one.
  • The habits of eight years in regulated science: measure everything, document everything.
  • A structured route through the exact gap your feedback named: agentic systems, APIs, MCPs.
  • Coaching and assessment that sharpen how I work and how I communicate.
  • Local and settled: relocating to Manchester this month, minutes from Knutsford.
  • One company, joined properly. I am looking to build somewhere long term, not pass through.

06 The funding

How the funding works

The training itself costs £18,000, met through the UK apprenticeship system rather than from PortSwigger's budget. There are two possible scenarios, and both are simple.

A

If PortSwigger pays the apprenticeship levy

UK payroll above £3m a year

Levy employers already set aside 0.5% of payroll into a training pot, money that is use it or lose it. The £18,000 draws down from that existing pot: funds already earmarked for exactly this purpose.

New training spend: £0
B

If PortSwigger is below the levy threshold

Non-levy employer

Government funds 95% of the training directly. The remaining 5%, at most £900 across the whole programme, is frequently covered in full by a levy transfer from a larger employer.

Typical training spend: £0 to £900
In either scenario the training is effectively already paid for. PortSwigger's real commitment is a genuine wage for genuine work, and that is the part I intend to earn.

Live AI drop-in clinics

Specialist AI trainers, live sessions four days a week, for sense-checking real use cases and decisions.

A working partnership

Your line manager and the development coach work together: monthly reviews, shared visibility, a clear picture of progress.

A Level 4 qualification on completion, with eligibility for RITTech professional registration.
